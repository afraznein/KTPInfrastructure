We're convening twelve companies of Day of Defeat operators on the basement floor of the TAP Esport Center, Philadelphia — the same Chinatown room that hosted the 2025 LAN. Three days of competition: the last day of July through the first weekend of August, 2026. Whether you're old guard, new blood, or somewhere bewildered in between, this is your dossier. Read it. Sign it. Bring it with you.

The exercise runs across three days. Friday opens with the draft event — a pickup tournament open to anyone in attendance who wants in, with seats limited at the door. Saturday and Sunday belong to team play: the registered companies bracket up and engage to a verdict, finals Sunday evening.

Inside this dossier you'll find your order of battle (who's pre-registered and with whom), your communiqué (when those engagements happen — bracket to follow), your terrain (where to find the building and what's nearby once you do), your rules of engagement (what's a fair shot and what's a forfeit), and the discipline expected of you under fire.

— Get ready for a weekend of fun, sweat, and dod innit.

VI

Rules of Engagement

PENDING VERIFICATION AGAINST ARCHIVE

1. General Conduct

1.1
LAN admins reserve the right to modify any rules as needed at any time as deemed necessary to the good continuation of the tournament.
1.2
Matches require the correct map, configuration file, and LAN server. No exceptions.
1.3
Server crash protocol. If the server goes down within the first five minutes of a half, the half is restarted in full. After five minutes, the half resumes with score restored and clock rounded up. No substitutions during recovery.
1.4
Pixel-walking forbidden. Accessing an area via pixel walking, occupying an area only reachable by pixel walking (e.g., harrington ladder), or hanging in such an area results in suspension and/or forfeiture.
1.5
Tied matches proceed to two five-minute overtime halves. Failure to complete overtime rounds results in a loss for both teams.
1.7
Seating assignments are final. LAN staff may modify seating as needed for operational reasons.

2. Cheating, Penalties & Disputes

2.1–2.3
Admins investigate suspicious activity. Matches involving cheaters may be overturned.
2.4
Prohibited tooling. Visual player-location aids, recoil-control software, target-acquisition software, illegally modified game files, null binds, monitor or software crosshairs, and mouse-acceleration programs.
2.5
Only default game files may be used unless explicitly approved or provided by LAN staff. Approved scoreboard modifiers and custom crosshairs (except sniper-rifle crosshair) are allowed.
2.6
Spectating after a match goes live is considered cheating. Spec hopping is permitted only during full-map captures or stuck-player situations.

3. Player Rules

3.1–3.2
Players must pass file and cvar checks, record POV demos each half, screenshot scoreboards, and remain through match conclusion.
3.3
Player boosting is allowed so long as the destination area is accessible by a single player without boosting.
3.4
Unauthorized voice commands and excessive opponent negging are prohibited.
3.5–3.8
Prohibited binds: actions coupled with attack, duck-scrollwheel macros, automated attack or movement scripts, and any weapon-spawning exploits.

4. Captain Rules

4.1–4.3
Captains: enhance scoreboard screenshot legibility, provide rcon status screenshots, and submit final scores with documentation to the shared drive.

5. Pauses

5.1–5.2
Each team receives one two-minute pause per half, callable only immediately after a cap-out.
5.3–5.4
Only captains may initiate pauses, with the exception of technical crashes (any team member may call).
5.5
Unauthorized-pause penalties: first offense awards opposing team cap-out points; second offense results in immediate match forfeiture.

VII

Code of Conduct

ADULT COMPETITORS — IN GOOD FAITH

0. Purpose

—
This Code of Conduct exists to ensure a fair, respectful, and enjoyable experience for all participants. As adult competitors, we expect players to uphold the highest standards of integrity, sportsmanship, and professionalism throughout the weekend.

1. Sportsmanship & Respect

1.1
While toxic behavior is common in this community online, let's strive to do better in person. Treat all players, officials, and spectators with respect. Harassment, abuse, or discriminatory behavior — verbal, written, or physical — will not be tolerated.
1.2
Practice good sportsmanship. Healthy competition is encouraged, but win or lose, do so with dignity. Excessive taunting, gloating, or inflammatory behavior is prohibited. A little is normal in this community — keep it within reason.
1.3
We are all here to have a good time, connect with others, and be competitive. Be considerate of the people around you.

2. Fair Play

2.1
No cheating or exploiting of any kind. The LAN follows the KTP rules wherever applicable, including Nein's CVAR checker on all LAN servers.
2.2
Use of third-party software, scripts, exploits, or tampering with game files — outside of approved files submitted to LAN organizers prior to the tournament — is grounds for immediate disqualification.

3. Conduct During Matches

3.1
Follow all match rules and schedules. Be on time and ready to play. Chronic lateness may result in penalties or forfeits. Group-stage schedule will be posted prior to the tournament; knock-out stage prior to leaving for the day. Be responsible for your team and know when your matches will take place.
3.2
To keep the tournament on time, matches will start no later than 10 minutes after their scheduled start, regardless of how many players are ready. Teams can choose to forfeit at that time if they don't have a full squad. Ringers are not allowed during tournament play except in extenuating circumstances at tournament-staff discretion.
3.3
Be mindful of noise levels and behavior. Keep noise at a reasonable level so everyone can enjoy the gaming experience.
3.4
Stream sniping is prohibited. Using information not available to players within their (or their teammates') point of view, in a way that gains unfair advantage, can result in forfeit of the match.
3.5
Mumble is the sole authorized comms tool. Using any tool outside of Mumble or in-game voice is prohibited.
3.6
Follow instructions from Tournament Staff. They are here to ensure everyone has a good time and to maintain order. Respect their decisions — their decisions are final.
3.7
Players are required to use the alias they signed up with, or some variation thereof. Aliasing is prohibited.

4. Conduct Outside of Matches

4.1
Outside of matches, available PCs can be claimed and used at will for other activities. Be aware that PCs are not permanently assigned — be respectful of other attendees and allow others a chance to use the PCs.
4.2
No formal time limits on PC use — use your best judgement. Tournament Staff will intervene if necessary to free up PCs for others.

5. Equipment and LAN Etiquette

5.1
Handle equipment with care. Take care of shared equipment and respect the venue. Treat the space responsibly.
5.2
Be accountable for your own equipment. Neither the LAN Center nor Tournament Staff is responsible for any lost or stolen items. Bring a tote or box for your peripherals when not actively using a PC.
5.3
Clean up your area after using a PC — including when leaving for an extended period — so that others may have a chance to play. Ask Tournament Staff before leaving peripherals at a specific PC.
5.4
Be respectful of, and do not tamper with, others' equipment.
5.5
Use the network responsibly. Don't abuse bandwidth or download anything illegal.
5.6
Report any damaged or malfunctioning equipment to Tournament Staff immediately.
5.7
Admin access is available on the PCs, but limit any changes to those necessary for your peripheral use. All changes are subject to review by Tournament Staff. This includes "Snap Tap." No unauthorized software or hardware modifications. If unsure, ask an official before making changes.

6. Substance Use

6.1
We are here to have a good time — maintain control of yourself when consuming substances. Excessive rowdiness can result in expulsion from the LAN for the remainder of the day.
6.2
The LAN center has banned smoking inside. Respect their rules.
6.3
Vaping is allowed by the LAN center, but be considerate of others — keep excessive vaping outside.

7. Penalties for Violations

7.1
Tournament organizers reserve the right to issue the following based on severity and frequency of misconduct:
  • Verbal or written warnings
  • Match or round forfeiture
  • Temporary or permanent removal from the tournament
  • Ban from future events

8. Agreement

8.1
By participating in this tournament, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct. Ignorance of the rules is not a valid defense. Let's work together to foster a competitive but respectful community.
Operative copy. The Code of Conduct above is published verbatim from the WSDoD command archive; any future amendments will be reflected here on the next document refresh.
