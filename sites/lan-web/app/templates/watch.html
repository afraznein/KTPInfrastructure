Caster's Field Manual — put the overlay on your stream new to OBS? open this

The on-screen graphics — scores, flags, timers, kill feed — are a transparent web page you lay over your game video in OBS. First time doing this? These steps take about two minutes.

  1. Have your game video in OBS already: a Window or Display Capture of your Day of Defeat spectator (HLTV) client. That is the picture — the overlay sits on top of it.
  2. In the Sources panel, click +, choose Browser, name it “KTP Overlay”, and click OK.
  3. In the URL field, paste: https://watch.ktpdod.com/screen?server=KTP%20LAN%201 — change the 1 on the end to whichever server you are casting (2, 3, 4, or 5).
  4. Set Width to 1920 and Height to 1080 to match your stream resolution. Leave everything else as-is and click OK.
  5. In the Sources list, drag “KTP Overlay” above your game capture so it draws on top. Its background is see-through — only the graphics show.
  6. Done. To cast a different server, double-click the source and change the server= number in the URL — or add one Browser source per server and switch between them.

TIP — right-click the overlay source → Properties → tick “Refresh browser when scene becomes active” so it always loads clean.
BLANK OVERLAY? — the graphics only fill in while a match is live, and the address must be exact (https, and %20 for the spaces in the server name).
