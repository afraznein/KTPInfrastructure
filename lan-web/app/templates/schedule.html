Why this schedule — strength of schedule

The six rounds aren't drawn at random. Each company plays six of the other nine, and we picked those six so every team's slate is as even as possible. Across the field the average opponent strength is near-identical — we measured the spread and it's roughly a third of what a naive draw produces. No company runs a gauntlet while another coasts.

Two more rules are baked in: no rematches, and the top seeds are kept apart through the early rounds. So Sunday's seeding reflects how teams actually played across the full slate — not who they happened to draw. A team can't back into a bye on a soft schedule, and a strong team won't miss one for catching every contender early.
